# Pitch Deck / Investor Pitch Playbook

## The first slide is the headline
One sentence that would survive being printed on a billboard. "We make [X] for [Y] so they can [Z]." Investors decide whether to keep listening in 15 seconds — the first slide is the entire pitch in miniature.

## Problem before solution, always
Spend a full minute on the problem before mentioning what you built. If the audience doesn't feel the problem, your solution sounds like a feature in search of a market. Use one specific story — a single customer, named, with a number.

## Numbers that prove the wedge
Pick three numbers and only three: market size, traction (revenue or users), and one efficiency metric (CAC payback, gross margin, retention curve). Investors will ask about the others — you don't need them in the deck.

## Pacing — vary deliberately
Investor pitches reward dynamic pacing more than speeches. Go fast on context, slow down on the ask. The pace shift is what signals "this part is the important part." Aim for 140–160 average with deliberate 90 WPM moments at the key claims.

## The competition slide
Never say "we have no competitors." It signals "no market." Show two real competitors and the axis on which you win. The axis matters more than the competitor names — investors are buying your strategic insight, not your market research.

## The ask is a sentence
Close with: "We're raising $X to do Y by Z." Number, purpose, timeline. Not "we're exploring funding" — investors don't fund explorations.
