# Impromptu Speaking Playbook

## The three-second pause
Before you start, pause three seconds. It feels like an hour to you. It feels like a confident gathering of thought to the audience. The pause is the single highest-leverage move in impromptu speaking.

## The PREP framework
Point, Reason, Example, Point. Make your point. Give one reason it's true. Give one concrete example. Restate the point. 30–45 seconds. Works for almost any "what do you think about X" question.

## Buying time without buying time
Don't say "that's a great question" — it telegraphs that you need time and didn't get any. Say "the way I think about that is…" — same time-buying effect, sounds like analysis.

## Concrete beats abstract every time
If you're forced to be general, ground every claim in one specific example. "Communication is important" is forgettable. "Last Tuesday my team shipped the wrong button colour because I hadn't sent the spec" is memorable. Concrete sells.

## Knowing when to stop
The fastest way to look in control is to stop earlier than you think. Make your point, give your example, restate, stop. Trailing off into "…and yeah, that's basically my thought on it" undoes the previous 30 seconds of confidence.

## Handling "I don't know"
Three honest options: "I don't know — let me think about it and come back to you", "I don't know the full answer but here's the part I do know", or "I don't know — what do you think?" All three are stronger than bluffing.
