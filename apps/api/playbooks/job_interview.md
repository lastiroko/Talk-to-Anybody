# Job Interview Playbook

## The "tell me about yourself" answer
60–90 seconds. Three beats: where you are now, two or three things you've shipped that connect to this role, why you're talking to this company specifically. End on the role. If you end on yourself, you've left the interviewer with "okay… and?"

## STAR for behavioural questions
Situation (10 seconds), Task (10 seconds), Action (60 seconds — the meat), Result (20 seconds, with a number if possible). Most candidates spend too long on Situation. Get to Action fast — that's where the signal lives.

## Pacing — moderate, not rushed
130–150 WPM. Interview nerves push everyone faster; calibrate by listening for your own breath. If you can't hear yourself breathing between sentences, you're going too fast.

## The honest-gap answer
When you don't know something: name what you do know, then say what you'd do to close the gap. "I haven't shipped X in production, but I've done Y and Z which are adjacent — here's how I'd approach it." Bluffing collapses on the follow-up; the honest-gap answer wins respect.

## Questions for them
End every interview with two specific questions about the team's actual work — not perks, not "what's the culture like." "What does a typical AI use case look like from kickoff to ship?" signals you've thought about the work.

## Closing — explicit interest
If you want the job, say it. "I'd love to do this work — what are the next steps from your side?" Implicit interest reads as ambivalence. Explicit interest reads as decisive, which is what interviewers project onto your future job performance.
